There are 5 PDF files here which you the operator/coordinator will find interesting. 

First is the ARRL Field Resource Manual, which will aid you in getting ready and 
deploying to a scene for emergency operations. This scene will most likely be in 
your town or general area, unless you opt for deploying to another region to assist 
their on going emergency.

Next ARRL manual to read would be the Amateur Radio Emergency Service manual. 
Chock full of good information and well worth the time to get an overall feel for the 
ARES service that we provide.

An older version of the ARES Manual is here, but the one above is newer.

Here is the ARRL Amateur Radio Emergency Communications Course. It is 
10 years old, but reading it will get you in the right frame of mind if you decide 
to take the ARRL EC-01 course for $50.
