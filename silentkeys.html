Northeast Missouri Amateur Radio Club

Serving Adair County and the Surrounding Area

This page serves as a memorial to NEMO ARC club members that have passed.


Bud Siebold

AA0JT

Donna Seibold

ABOPH

Bud and Donna Siebold were members of our club for many years.

They lived in Alaska at the time of the Alaska earthquake of 1964 and had many stories about what that disaster was like.
They farmed west of laplata and could always be relied on to be part of club meetings and events.

Bud loved working CW and would encourage others to learn and use the code.

Vance Myers

KD0SGU

Vance could always be relied on for a joke.

Many will remember his wearing a sombrero to the club meeting on the 5th of may one year.

Vance owned Airco heating and cooling and was well known in the community.

Mel Wheeler

WD0FRY

Mel was another long time member of our club. 
He was from the Lancaster area and worked as a proprane deliveryman for many years.
Mel had an inventor's spirit and came up with many ingeneous gadgets for ham radio.

He is fondly remembered for driving a lawn mower with a trailer full of batteries to Field Day one year.


Patrick McGillan

KA9PDK

Patrick was a retired Air Force vet and was active in the airforce and army MARS program.

A past president of the club. He was very involved in his community. 

Patrick could always be relied on to help out when the club had an activity.
















Dave Newman






KB0NWT












Dave was a past president of the NemoARC

Always with a ready laugh Dave never seemed gruff or down.

A fond memory of him was when he was operating at the first field day I attended as a new ham.  

He was happily willing to show a new guy some of the ins and outs of being an amateur. 












AB0DK's memories of KB0NWT









































Charles Porter II



WB0ZHB



Chuck was a Kirksville native. He was involved in a wide variety of interests. 



Including Model Railroads, Boy Scouts, and wood carving.  













As a past president of the club, he used his long history of amateur radio experience for the betterment of the club.
From hosting field day to presenting storm spotter presentations, Chuck could always be counted on to support the club and ham radio members.
